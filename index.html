My name is Jesse Mitchell. I'm a Petroleum Geologist with 11 years experience in the E&P industry. About a year ago I started hearing a lot about this thing called "Data Science" and started exploring what it was all about. It turns out there is a significant overlap between Petroleum Geology and Data Science workflows: gathering, integrating, and cleaning data, reshaping it into a usable format and then analyzing the data with the goals of generating new opportunities, mitigating risk, and increasing productivity. While some data science techniques were completely new to me (analyzing twitter threads for example) other areas (data cleaning, statistical and numerical analysis) were very familiar. Whether looking at petrophysical studies, risk analysis, optimization of completion techniques, play analysis or production optimization, there are numerous applications.

The Petroleum Industry as a whole is integrating more and more data science concepts. Data Vendors that used to just sell raw data are putting out new data analytics products and tools to help E&P companies move more quickly, and the E&P companies themselves are bringing on more data scientists and analysts to help extract value from huge amounts of industry data. Tools such as Spotfire and Tableau are used much more extensively than they were just a few years ago and I believe the trend will continue with further adoption of R, Python and other Data Science tools. I decided to follow this idea to see where it leads, and I've recently been devoting most of my free time to building my skillset with Python, R, and SQL.

I put this website together with the goal of documenting my progress. So far the majority of this project has invovled getting up to speed with R and Python and the numerous libraries available for data analysis: pandas, numpy, dplyr, tidyr, ggplot, matplotlib, scikit-learn... PyPI contains 87327 packages for Python! Thankfully you don't need quite that many for data analysis applications. Please take a look at my blog or some of the links to the left to see what I've been working on. The projects I have posted so far all invovle Open Source data, but I look forward to the chance to work on datasets in Petroleum and other industries. Thanks for visiting!
